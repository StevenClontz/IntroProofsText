The subset order from Example 5.5.7 is generally not a total order on \(S\text{.}\) For example, let \(S=\{1, 2, 3\}\text{.}\) Then for the sets \(A=\{1\}\) and \(B=\{2\}\text{,}\) \(A\nsubseteq B\) and \(B\nsubseteq A\text{.}\) Thus neither \(A\) nor \(B\) is “smaller” than the other and they cannot be ordered relative to each other with the subset order. Similarly, if \(C=\{2, 3\}\text{,}\) \(A\nsubseteq C\) and \(C\nsubseteq A\text{.}\) Once again, we cannot order these sets by \(\subseteq\text{.}\)
