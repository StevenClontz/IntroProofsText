To prove there exists a unique \(x\in D\) such that \(P(x)\text{:}\)
  1. Produce a candidate. This means you state the \(x\) that makes \(P(x)\) true. You may need to do work to find \(x\text{,}\) but that work is not part of the actual proof.
  2. Show the candidate works. In other words, show \(P(x)\) is true. This shows existence.
  3. Assume \(P(x)\) is true and \(P(y)\) is true. Show \(x=y\text{.}\) This shows uniqueness.
