If we check the conditions of Theorem 5.5.11, we can see again that the subset order from Example 5.5.7 is generally not a total order on \(S\text{.}\) Let \(S=\{1, 2, 3\}\text{.}\) Then for the sets \(A=\{1\}\) and \(B=\{2\}\text{,}\) \(A\nsubseteq B\text{,}\) \(B\nsubseteq A\text{,}\) and \(A\neq B\text{.}\) Similarly, if \(C=\{2, 3\}\text{,}\) \(A\nsubseteq C\text{,}\) \(C\nsubseteq A\) and \(A\neq C\text{.}\)
