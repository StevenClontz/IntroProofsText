Let \(r, s\in \mathbb{Q}\) with \(r < s\text{.}\) Let \(x=\frac{r+s}{2}\) (this is stating the candidate). Then \(x\) is rational (see ExerciseÂ 3.2.3.5 or prove this as an exercise). Furthermore,
\begin{equation*} r=\frac{2r}{2}=\frac{r+r}{2} < \frac{r+s}{2} < \frac{s+s}{2} =\frac{2s}{2}=s. \end{equation*}
Thus, \(r < x < s\text{.}\) (We showed that \(x\) satisfied all the necessary properties.)
